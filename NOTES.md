My best configuration utilizes a 4-layer, 192-dimension transformer with 6 attention heads and tied weights, totaling approximately 1.87M parameters. It succeeded because it aggressively maximized the 2,000,000 parameter budget while respecting the limitations of a strict 2,000-step training ceiling. Doubling the context window to 512 proved detrimental, so keeping the `block_size` at 256 ensured stable gradient updates over the short run. A cosine annealing learning rate scheduler with a 150-step warmup allowed the AdamW optimizer to take massive initial steps (up to 2e-3) before settling into a stable minima. Increasing the batch size to 32 further stabilized these aggressive gradient updates. Finally, eliminating dropout entirely (0.0) proved essential; in a heavily constrained 2,000-step environment over a small dataset, underfitting is a far greater risk than overfitting, and the model needed 100% of its network capacity active at all times to drive the Bits Per Byte (bpb) down to 1.9457.
